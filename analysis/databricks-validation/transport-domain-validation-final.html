Executive Verdict

Overall assessment The domain model is directionally right. The major correction is evidence strength: some items are source-backed, some are interview-led only, and some are not validated by the source tables.
Keep Contract as the partitioning key; asset hierarchy; inspections; defects/hazards; jobs/work orders; KPIs; tunnel event handling; capital works as a distinct domain.
Challenge RetinaVision, AID camera flow, dashcam frequency, GeoJSON handover, fleet telematics and the exact SLA calculation engine are not proven by the source-table validation.
Do not make the document sound more certain than the evidence allows. Where a claim comes from interviews, label it as interview-backed. Where the table evidence is absent or weak, do not treat it as confirmed.

Corrected wording

Change the method examples to: manual / field inspection / Asset Vision-style inspection forms / tunnel maintenance or event forms where present. Defects & Hazards

Corrected wording
Defects and hazards are best modelled together as work-generating events, with severity where populated. In vjob, HazardDefectCode is the reliable common field; HazardCode is materially patchier and absent in some catalogues.
What changes
Keep asset link, defect/hazard type, raised date, location and photo evidence. Change Auto-calculated SLA / response time based on severity + asset category to SLA due date and compliance outcome fields are present; the calculation rules are likely contract-scoped and were not proven from the source tables alone. Raised-from categories such as inspection, public report and incidental observation should be retained only where source forms or interviews support them.
Evidence level
Source-backed Defect/hazard jobs, due dates, compliance, completion, made-safe flags, WKT and photo evidence are supported.

Live Databricks Findings Used

Finding Evidence from validation run
Source catalogues have the expected Asset Vision core tables. Validated catalogues contain tables/views such as asset, inspection, job, capitalwork, photo, module, formfield, workflowstatus, vjob, vinspection and vcapitalwork.
Contract is a real partitioning key. Live counts show large contract populations: WRU, SRAP-C, TSRC, RAMC Gen2, Brisbane Airport, Port of Brisbane, SHT/WHT, Auckland West Transport and VentureSmart among others.
Jobs/defects/hazards are strongly supported. vjob contains contract, asset ID, hazard/defect code, due date, compliance, completion, made-safe and WKT fields with large row counts across multiple catalogues.
Inspections are separate from jobs. vinspection has scheduled/completed dates, asset links, job links, capital-work links and WKT, but field completeness differs by contract.
Capital works is real. capitalwork and vcapitalwork have rows across validated source catalogues, with asset links, planned start, actual finish and spatial fields where populated.
Evidence/photos are significant. Photo tables include large volumes for Job Before, Job After, Job During, Inspection, Asset, CapitalWorkTask and form-field contexts.
OMCS/dTIMS/handover/as-built are partially validated. Precise source term query found OMCS-related tunnel form fields, dTIMS treatment/model fields, handover/practical completion fields and as-built mismatch references.
RetinaVision, AID camera flow, GeoJSON and telematics are not validated enough. Precise source term query found no useful RetinaVision or GeoJSON evidence, only weak AID evidence, and sparse telemetry/telematics evidence.

Recommended Final Wording

The Transport data asset should be modelled around a contract-scoped Asset Vision operating core: contract/network context, asset register and hierarchy, inspections, defects/hazards, jobs/work orders, capital works, evidence/media, KPIs/reporting and tunnel event handling. The source tables strongly support the asset, inspection, job, hazard/defect, SLA outcome, photo and capital works domains. Sensing and telemetry should be written carefully: OMCS, camera/CCTV, alarms, fans and AI Defect ID appear in source forms, but RetinaVision, AID-camera incident flow, dashcam capture intervals, GeoJSON handover and fleet telematics were not proven by the source-table validation and should remain interview-led until separate evidence is provided.
