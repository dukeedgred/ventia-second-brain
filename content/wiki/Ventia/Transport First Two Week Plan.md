# Transport First Two Week Plan

This source is a detailed mobilisation plan for the first two weeks of the Transport programme. It is a planning note rather than meeting minutes, and it sets out workstream anchors, stakeholder groups, interview waves, and the decision gate needed before week 3 begins.

## Summary

The plan frames the first two weeks around four anchors: an enterprise Transport data product, RAMC bid support, sensing and telemetry integration, and governance or operating-model setup. It connects the immediate discovery work to the broader [[Integrated Transport Data Asset]], [[Transport Data Landscape]], [[Transport Gen 3 Tender Innovation]], and [[Transport Asset Intelligence Roadmap]].

Week 1 is focused on sponsor alignment, stakeholder interviews, and current-state systems and data mapping. Week 2 shifts into sensing and RAMC deep dives, access-blocker resolution, validation, proof-of-concept selection, and roadmap definition.

## Key Details

- The enterprise Transport data product work needs to map core and adjacent data domains, source systems, ownership, access paths, and reusable asset patterns.
- The RAMC bid work needs a risk-based road maintenance narrative, bid proof points, and an initial operational decision use case.
- The sensing and telemetry workstream names Retina Vision, BYD telemetry, drainage IoT, weather data, traffic data, and open road datasets as candidate inputs.
- Governance work needs working groups, decision rights, access blockers, meeting evidence, and a clean handoff into weeks 3 to 6.
- The decision gate should agree the proof point, owner, data access, and week 3 commencement conditions.

## Stakeholder Waves

The plan groups interviews into four waves: sponsor and bid stakeholders, operational proof-point stakeholders, data/governance/access stakeholders, and validation or external-data stakeholders. Named stakeholders and their groups are consolidated on [[Engagement Team]].
