# Ventia AI and Innovation Portfolio

The DAII monthly meeting showcased a broad AI and innovation portfolio spanning work winning, field operations, data engineering, safety, and employee innovation. The portfolio connects to [[Ventia Data Governance Framework]] because many initiatives depend on trusted data, controls, and repeatable delivery patterns.

## Investor Day Showcase

Ventia's DAII team had a prominent Investor Day showcase in the Victoria Cross lobby. The meeting described it as the first Ventia video produced using the refreshed brand guidelines, with strong investor engagement around the team's AI, data, and technology work.

Edge Red created visuals to bring VenSpark data to life in a more engaging way than a standard Power BI report.

## VenIQ

VenIQ was presented as an AI-powered bid and commercial optimisation capability for work winning. It ingests tenders, extracts scope, requirements, and evaluation criteria, links them to Ventia's historical bids, suppliers, and cost drivers, and builds a knowledge graph that strengthens with each submission.

The intended value is to increase bid capacity, improve win rates, surface cross-sell opportunities, and provide pricing and commercial intelligence without matching headcount growth.

## AI Apprentice

AI Apprentice was presented as a voice- and vision-enabled assistant for field workers. It can help workers confirm jobs, upload photos, surface safety risks and recommended controls, consolidate client and building-code requirements into site reports, recommend suppliers by scope, cost, and performance, and raise purchase orders into Ventia's procurement system.

## Safety And Innovation Initiatives

- Apple Watch trial: 18 Ventia employees are trialling Apple Watch Ultra devices for health monitoring, backtrack for lone workers, and satellite-over-voice capability.
- Beklar safety app: a US safety app is being trialled with phone and watch support, geotagged check-ins, duress functionality, false-alarm cancellation, and alerts to loved ones, Ventia VOC, and safety leads.
- VenSpark: a company-wide campaign had more than 500 ideas and 1,500 likes, with a push for continued engagement over the remaining 130 business days before year-end shutdown.
- SWIMS AI: an automation concept for Safe Work Method Statements that could check submissions against critical risk protocols and state-level requirements, then return required corrections automatically.

## Transport Innovation Themes

The Transport Data and AI Working Group source adds bid-facing innovation themes for [[Transport Gen 3 Tender Innovation]]. These include autonomous defect and hazard recognition, SAP/IBM/Nextspace demo work, IoT-enabled predictive maintenance, crowdsourced maintenance tasks, and AI decision support that combines historical data, real-time sensor inputs, and weather forecasts.

The source stresses that Transport innovation needs to be visual and actionable. The output should help teams kick off remediation projects, programs, work orders, or intervention backlog items rather than only provide a dashboard.
