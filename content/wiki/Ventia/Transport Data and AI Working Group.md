# Transport Data and AI Working Group

This source is an internal kickoff email for the Transport statement of work, with embedded notes from an Evolve/RAMC discussion. It identifies stakeholder groups, Transport asset-data needs, tender-driven innovation themes, and open questions about how SAP, Retina Vision, Nextspace, Asset Vision, and related visualisation tools should fit together.

## Summary

The email proposes a stakeholder map for the Transport SOW across data SMEs, digital Transport SMEs, pre-contract and strategy contacts, Transport SLT, prior innovation contacts, and the data engineering team. It also captures input from John Parisella on defects and attributes that need to be captured for VRMC, RAMC, and most roads contracts.

The embedded transcript focuses on RAMC/Gen 3 tender innovation, where TMR is expected to value AI, technology uplift, autonomous defect and hazard recognition, clearer visualisation, and credible roadmaps. The discussion links [[Transport Asset Intelligence Roadmap]] needs to [[Transport Gen 3 Tender Innovation]], [[Asset Vision]], [[Transport Data Landscape]], and [[Ventia Data Platform Modernisation]].

## Key Details

- The Transport SOW should involve data SMEs, digital Transport SMEs, pre-contract/work-winning/strategy contacts, Transport SLT, operational contacts, prior innovation leads, and data engineering.
- Defect capture needs include cracking, potholes, foliage, sign damage, crocodile cracking, shoving, rutting, raveling, flushing, edge damage, faded line marking, kerb and channel, barriers, litter, vegetation, ponding water, sweeping, roadkill, graffiti, guideposts, and future tunnel assets.
- Outputs need to be geolocated and flow into visualisation or action tools such as Maximo, SAP, [[Asset Vision]], and Nextspace.
- Required outputs include location, defect data, duplicate capture detection, patterns, and work order details.
- The note states there is currently no connection between Retina Vision and [[Asset Vision]], which is described as lower-hanging integration work before SAP.
- The Evolve/RAMC discussion considered a demo using SAP, IBM, and Nextspace, with real data and a clear story for the tender submission.
- The transcript also raised future technology themes: IoT sensors, predictive maintenance, crowdsourced maintenance tasks, AI decision support, and SAP Asset Performance Management.

## Open Questions

- How should SAP, Retina Vision, Nextspace, [[Asset Vision]], SAP S/4HANA, SAP SAC, and SAP Asset Performance Management fit together?
- Can the team build a working prototype across Retina Vision, Nextspace, SAP S/4HANA, and SAP SAC within the tender timeframe?
- Will traffic type and traffic volume data be available for predictive or prioritisation use cases?
- What data can be shared with Nextspace, and what NDA or probity controls are required?
- The transcript refers to real "Ramsey" data for the demo; confirm whether this means RAMC data.
