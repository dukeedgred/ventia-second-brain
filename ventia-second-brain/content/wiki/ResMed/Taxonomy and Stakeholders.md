# Taxonomy and Stakeholders

The category taxonomy used to classify indirect spend, and the stakeholders who own or review it.

## Taxonomy Principles

- Classification targets L1–L4 category levels.
- Vague labels such as **"others"** or **"general"** are to be avoided — taxonomy definitions are being refined to prevent these catchall categories.
- A **taxonomy suggestion column** has been added to AI output so each classification carries an explicit reasoning trace.

## Unclassifiable Items

Items that lack sufficient context in the invoice description are flagged explicitly rather than assigned a vague category. See [[Classification Approach]] for how each method handles these.

## Key Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| Mia | IT category manager; UAT reviewer for IT spend categories |
| Finance team | Sign-off on L1–L4 taxonomy definitions |
| Procurement team | Taxonomy alignment and buy-in |

## Supplier Data as Classification Signal

Supplier names and **GL codes** provide additional context for classifying spend where descriptions are vague. The AI currently considers supplier data but its impact has been inconsistent — further optimisation planned.
