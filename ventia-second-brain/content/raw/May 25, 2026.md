Mini User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Planning: The team discussed an upcoming mini UAT session with Mia, the IT category manager, targeted for Tuesday or Wednesday. Charlotte Pan noted the necessity of preparing the results—including artificial intelligence (AI), large language model (LLM), and rule-based determinations—to share with Mia for review, acknowledging that Dave’s absence may slightly delay the timeline.
RAG and LLM Evaluation Strategy: Honglin Jin shared updates regarding progress on retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) tasks, including the review of code and the integration of an LLM as a judge for accuracy calculations. The team agreed to brainstorm methods for combining rule-based, RAG, and LLM outputs, as a definitive consensus on the integration strategy has not yet been reached.
Taxonomy and Unclassifiable Data: Charlotte Pan and the team explored the need to refine taxonomy definitions to prevent vague classifications, such as "others" or "general". Junaid Dewan explained that current rule-based approaches flag unclassifiable items, such as descriptions lacking sufficient context, and that these items are excluded from the classification results to maintain accuracy. The team agreed that the AI should be tasked with flagging unclassifiable items by evaluating invoice descriptions.
Leveraging Supplier Data for Classification: Junaid Dewan proposed using supplier-related data points and General Ledger (GL) codes to identify trends, which could assist in classifying expenditures where descriptions are vague. Honglin Jin and Charlotte Pan acknowledged that while the AI currently considers supplier information, its impact on classification has sometimes been inconsistent, and further discussions are needed to optimize this approach.
Streamlit App Development: Duke Kang reported that work is focused on finalizing the user interface for the Streamlit application to demonstrate to Erikson and Benjamin. The team plans to create mock tables in Snowflake to establish necessary connections for the application.
Sample Selection and Execution: The team finalized a plan to use a stratified sample of 400 records for the upcoming UAT, with the possibility of increasing the sample size if required. Junaid Dewan and Duke Kang will populate rules and apply them to the entire dataset, which will then be joined to Honglin Jin's base table.
Database Access and Interim Workarounds: Charlotte Pan noted that write access to Snowflake for the team has not yet been confirmed by Benji. To meet the Tuesday deadline for sharing outputs with Mia, the team decided to produce file extracts rather than waiting for direct database access, a method that Junaid Dewan confirmed is feasible.
Reporting Accuracy and Evaluation Framework: The team discussed the need for a robust evaluation framework as the project moves toward a mid-July completion. Honglin Jin will append determination columns to the current spreadsheet to update accuracy reporting, with plans to provide breakdowns by subcategory or price range to build user confidence. Charlotte Pan requested a brainstorming session to finalize the evaluation approach, which will assess how to trust and validate results across the different classification methods.
